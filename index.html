01 — Executive Summary

Five Findings at a Glance

The decline in positive Share of Voice is not a brand output failure. It is a denominator crisis. Hostile third-party content has flooded every major digital channel simultaneously, mathematically diluting Adani's stable positive messaging into single-digit relevance on some platforms.

01
The Denominator Exploded
Total Instagram + YouTube conversation volume grew 259% — from ~37,300 trackable mentions to over 134,000. Positive brand content stayed flat. The math is brutal: same numerator, 3× denominator = collapse.
02
Three Domestic Flashpoints
Smart meter billing outrage (65K mentions), Vedanta/Jaiprakash insolvency bias (28.5K), and Congress's Nicobar ecological campaign (42K) each independently triggered mass engagement.
03
International Validation Loop
OFAC's $275M sanctions settlement and the DOJ bribery case dismissal gave domestic critics credible foreign authority to cite — amplifying every local narrative simultaneously.
04
Sentiment Radicalization
Negative sentiment on Instagram escalated from 45.1% to 79.3%; on YouTube from 48.6% to 82.1%. Combined platform negative share now exceeds 80%. The audience is no longer skeptical — it is hostile.
05
Budget Parity is Insufficient
Maintaining baseline spend in a 3× larger, algorithmically hostile environment guarantees further SOV erosion. Volume, localization, and pre-bunking must scale to match the new denominator.
📌
The Core Mechanism
SOV is a ratio: Positive Mentions ÷ Total Mentions. Adani's positive messaging (the numerator) remained broadly stable. The total Instagram + YouTube conversation (the denominator) nearly tripled. A stable numerator divided by an exploding denominator always approaches zero. This is not a campaign quality problem. It is a volume equilibrium problem that requires a corresponding volume response.

06 — Strategic Implications

What Must Change

The media environment has structurally mutated. Maintaining baseline spend guarantees further SOV erosion. The organisation must exponentially scale positive output — and change what it says, to whom, and where.

⚠ RECOMMENDATION PRIORITY: URGENT
01 — Volume Response
Scale to Match the New Denominator

Maintaining the same budget as last year in a 3× larger hostile environment is mathematically insufficient. To recover from 14.2% back to ~30% positive SOV, positive content volume must at minimum triple — and sustain, not spike. Sporadic bursts will be algorithmically overwhelmed by continuous hostile flow.

02 — Localise the Response
The Smart Meter Crisis Needs Hyperlocal Counter-Deployment

The smart meter narrative is a microeconomic household issue. Generic pan-India brand content cannot address a consumer in Colaba whose bill tripled. Counter-deployment must be pincode-level: regional-language creators, direct bill dispute resolution content, and localised social proof from satisfied consumers. Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu require independent strategies.

03 — Pre-Bunking over Rebuttal
Get Ahead of the Next Narrative Before it Breaks

Rebuttal campaigns launched after a hostile narrative peaks are ineffective — the audience has already formed fixed beliefs. Pre-bunking (proactively addressing likely attack vectors) is evidentially more effective. The Nicobar ecological narrative is still growing: proactive environmental counter-content deployed now reaches an audience that has not yet hardened against Adani.

04 — Creator Architecture
Two Proven Archetypes Have Organic Credibility

The Educational Loophole: UPSC/EdTech creators (Mrunal Patel, StudyIQ) can package infrastructure achievement as objective civics content — reaching 30M+ education-oriented subscribers with zero branded toxicity.

The Finance Realist: Creators like CA Rahul Malodia who discuss Adani's business mechanics factually achieve higher trust than any CSR-adjacent campaign.

🎯
The Core Recommendation
Maintaining spend at last year's level is insufficient in the current environment because hostile conversation volume has increased 239%. The key action: increase the volume and consistency of positive / narrative-shaping content to regain Share of Voice and offset the rise in negative external conversation. Budget parity guarantees continued erosion. Only volume parity — or better, volume advantage — can arrest the denominator effect.
